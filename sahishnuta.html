--- title: Sahishnuta ---

Sahishnuta: An illuminated tale of a great love Sahishnuta is an interactive book, a sculpture and a lamp that tells one of the important tales in Indian history. It illuminates as you unfold the story of Jodhaa and Akbar, who united two warring kingdoms, despite their different religious beliefs.

During the 16th century, the Mughal empire aggressively captured and proselytized Indian kingdoms under the leadership of Emperor Jalal ud-din.

Rajput King of Amer, Bharmal requests Jalal to marry his daughter, Jodhaa in an attempt to save his kingdom and people from the Mughal Army.

Princess Jodhaa, a devout follower of Lord Krishna wishes to retain her faith after marriage, to which Jalal kindly accepts.

Unhappy with the interfaith alliance, Jalal's foster mother, Maham Anga sends a false letter to Jodhaa's cousin brother that Jodhaa is in trouble.

Sujamal rushes to save his sister at the middle of a night. Jalal estranges Jodhaa to see her meeting a man in private at the odd hour.

Later, Jalal realizes his mistake but Jodhaa wouldn’t return unless he learns to win the hearts of people, to which Jalal goes incognito.

Jalal fights with the religious heads of his court to immediately abolish the Pilgrim Tax that was being collected from people of other faiths.

Delighted at Jalal’s kindness, Jodhaa reunites with him. His people sing him praises and present the title,"Akbar" (The Great).
